2025: A Better Care Future

Far from portraying a glossy utopia where machines seamlessly assume responsibility for gaps in social care, 2025: A Better Care Future portrays a more realistic – maybe even mundane – 'frictionful' near future in which technology itself doesn’t quite work and needs human support.

A Digital Tomorrow

"A Digital Tomorrow" is a design fiction video produced for Curious Rituals, a research project conducted in July-August 2012 by Nicolas Nova (The Near Future Laboratory / HEAD-Genève), Katherine Miyake, Nancy Kwon and Walton Chiu from the media design program. This research project was about gestures, postures and digital rituals that typically emerged with the use of digital technologies (computers, mobile phones, sensors, robots, etc.).

Fly Me To The Moon aka Dinner with Friends

Four Friends pay for dinner in the future using integrated mobile payments, credit cards, private coins and corporate space currency; while their waiter is saving/earning enough space currency to win a sub-orbit space trip.

Shadowed City

This film is the final deliverable of Speculative Design studio class at MFA Transdisciplinary design at Parsons School of design. In this film, they presented an imagined life of living in the future New York City, where there is a significant lack of sunlight at street level due to the continous uprising of super tall buildings.

Spatial Bodies: Hong Kong & Shenzhen

Commissioned by the UABB 2019, AUJIK's latest work Spatial Bodies: Shen Zhen & HongKong(2019) is exhibited in MOCAUP "Ascending City". AUJIK applies a conceptual approach to Shenzhen, re-creating the architectural domain to something that resembles electronic components and circuits which are interconnected like a huge computer motherboard.

Year 1999 A D

In the "1999 House of Tomorrow," each family member's activities are enabled by a central computer and revolve around products remarkably similar to those made by the sponsor. Power comes from a self-contained fuel cell, which supports environmental controls, an automatic cooking system, and a computer-assisted "education room."
